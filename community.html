Steve Cadigan, LinkedIn’s first CHRO, Joins TBrA’s Board of Directors

Steve Cadigan, a renowned talent strategist, author and LinkedIn’s first Chief Human Resources Officer, has joined TheBoardroom Africa as a Non-Executive Director.

With over 25 years of experience, Steve has led HR efforts at ESPRIT, Cisco Systems, and Electronic Arts, and played a pivotal role in guiding LinkedIn from a private firm to its successful IPO. He also advises top companies such as Airbnb, Intel, Salesforce, and the BBC, and his expertise is sought by venture capital firms such as Andreessen Horowitz and Sequoia.

Steve’s background and unique expertise sits at the intersection of talent, technology, and sustainability will be invaluable as we advance our mission to deliver top, diverse leadership talent at scale and provide the tools leaders need to transform their businesses.

Please join us in congratulating Steve on his appointment. We invite you to learn more about his work.

 Steve Cadigan
Futurist, Talent Strategist & Author

Josephine Olok becomes Chartered Director

Open Doors Alumna

Josephine Olok has successfully achieved Chartered Director status, a mark of leadership excellence. Her path to this prestigious credential started with TBrA’s Open Doors programme, developed in partnership with the Institute of Directors (IoD), UK.

Recognised globally, Chartered Director status is an exclusive qualification reserved for the world’s top business leaders and is the only director certification awarded under the Royal Charter. To secure this status, Josephine completed the Certificate in Company Direction through Open Doors, earned the Diploma in Company Direction, and further showcased extensive experience and strong acumen in directorial roles.

We are proud to celebrate Josephine’s milestone achievement and look forward to witnessing her drive more transformation in her leadership capacity.


Achieving Chartered Director status has been a challenging but incredibly rewarding experience, marking a significant milestone in my board career. The rigorous training, assessments and interviews in all aspects of board governance has enhanced my knowledge and understanding of governance principles and practices.

I have felt more confident to contribute and to add value to the boards that I serve, and I have found that my ability to navigate board challenges has improved.


Awarded: Certificate in Company Direction
