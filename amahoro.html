This month we spotlight Uganda, Africa’s largest refugee hosting country, to explore how progressive and inclusive policies can unlock the economic potential of refugees and forcibly displaced persons (FDPs). Our focus spans from the insights of the Pathways to Employment: Uganda report to inspiring stories from the hospitality sector. The evidence is clear: when supportive legal frameworks are matched with private sector initiative, refugees do more than participate. They thrive, contribute, and drive industry growth.

Pathways to Employment: Uganda Report


Uganda is recognized globally for its progressive refugee policies, offering displaced communities access to land, education, and work opportunities. This report takes a closer look at how these policies and the wider legal framework shape employment prospects for refugees, while also equipping businesses with the practical tools they need to engage. Through the Employers’ Toolkit, it provides guidance on how companies can confidently and compliantly hire refugee talent. By exploring the report, you’ll also find clear answers to practical questions: Which policies and legal frameworks guide refugees’ access to employment in Uganda? What challenges might employers encounter in the hiring process? And how can integrating refugee talent strengthen your business and the wider economy?

Introducing the Amahoro Skills Hub

The Skills Hub is Amahoro Coalition’s digital platform designed to connect refugees and forcibly displaced persons (FDPs) to meaningful economic opportunities across Africa.

Who is it for?

  • Job seekers (refugees, displaced persons, and students) looking for internships or employment opportunities.
  • Partners (NGOs, training institutions, and support organizations) who want to showcase and refer to the talent they support, while also tracking their transition into jobs or internships.

What does it do?

  • Posts available jobs and internships from across Africa, specifically targeting refugees and displaced persons.
  • Enables partners to open an account, upload their talent pool, and generate analytics reports to measure impact.
  • Allows partners to directly refer candidates to available jobs and monitor their transition into employment.
  • Helps Amahoro Coalition identify companies and sectors ready to absorb refugee and displaced talent.
