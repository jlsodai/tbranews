Bridging Tradition and Impact: The Evolution of High Net Worth Individual Philanthropy in Africa


 

Philanthropy has long been a cornerstone of African societies, rooted in deep traditions of communal support and mutual aid. Today, as Africa experiences unprecedented economic growth and a surge in High Net Worth Individuals (HNWIs), these values are evolving into structured, strategic giving with the potential to transform the continent. Yet, while Africa’s wealth is expanding - holding an estimated $2.5 trillion in investable assets—millions still face extreme poverty and food insecurity. Our new report explores the emerging role of African HNWIs in philanthropy, the barriers limiting its full potential, and the opportunities to create high-impact, locally driven solutions that bridge the gap between tradition and modern philanthropy.

We have partnered with Etihad Airways to launch a Community Development Centre in Nairobi, Kenya



Etihad Airways, in collaboration with the Amahoro Coalition, have established the Etihad Airways Community Development Centre in Nairobi, a groundbreaking initiative aimed at supporting underserved and forcibly displaced communities. This strategic partnership reflects a shared commitment to education, women’s empowerment, and creating sustainable livelihoods for marginalized populations.

The centerpiece of this development is a boarding school designed to serve children from nursery to grade 6. Additionally, the development will feature a vocational training centre dedicated to empowering young women with employability and entrepreneurial skills. Through practical training and skills development, the centre will also equip participants to build sustainable livelihoods and become active contributors to their communities.

Amahoro Coalition is a Founding Partner of the newly launched Displacement Film Fund at IFFR 2025



Amahoro Coalition is proud to be a founding partner of the Displacement Film Fund, a groundbreaking initiative launched at the 54th International Film Festival Rotterdam (IFFR) to support displaced filmmakers and authentic storytelling on forced displacement. Offering up to five production grants of €100,000, the Fund is backed by a coalition of film industry experts, business leaders, and philanthropists, with the ambition of creating a lasting impact in global cinema. First initiated at UNHCR’s Global Refugee Forum, this initiative aligns with Amahoro Coalition’s mission to elevate underrepresented voices and unlock opportunities for displaced communities. The selected filmmakers will be announced at the 2025 Cannes Film Festival, with their completed films premiering at IFFR 2026—marking a new chapter in the intersection of film, storytelling, and social impact.
