Norsad Capital

Non-Executive Director


Industry: Financial Services

Norsad was first established in 1990, a collaboration between the Nordic and Southern African Development Community (SADC) governments, and operates as a multilateral agency based in Lusaka, Zambia. In 2011, Norsad transformed into a limited liability company, Norsad Finance, incorporated in Botswana and accredited by the International Financial Services Centre. It rebranded to Norsad Capital in 2022 in line with the strategy to scale and deepen its impact in Africa.

Norsad Capital is an impact investor and private credit provider offering debt and mezzanine finance to mid-market growth companies in Africa. Its purpose is to build a better Africa by supporting the growth of profitable African companies through tailor-made debt finance solutions, enabling them to have substantial and ongoing social impact through the services and employment they provide. This is driven by an aspiration to positively impact the lives of 100 million Africans by 2030.

Norsad Capital is searching for 1 (one) Non-Executive Director to join their board as a replacement for a retired director.

The application deadline for this role is 31 July at midnight, 2023.

Financing Alliance for Health

Non-Executive Directors


Industry: Health Financing

Despite strong evidence for investments in community health systems in Sub-Saharan Africa, there is still a funding gap of over $4 billion. Current donor funding supports mostly vertical, disease-specific Community Health Worker (CHW) programmes that are costlier and less efficient than horizontal, integrated programs. National governments that seek to streamline and scale CHW programmes face significant barriers, including building political prioritisation, developing policies, mapping resources, and establishing financing strategies. As a result, Ministry of Health teams often struggle to mobilise resources and scale robust, sustainable community health systems with community health workers at their core.

Financing Alliance for Health (FAH) is working towards a new reality where domestic and international funders prioritise community health as a key contributor to achieving Universal Health Coverage and recognition of community health as a critical investment rather than a cost that burdens individual members of the society. To accomplish this, the Financing Alliance for Health partners with governments on all steps of financing to strengthen and sustain community health systems that are critically important for improving health outcomes, leveraging primary health care to attain universal health coverage.

Financing Alliance for Health is searching for 3 (three) Non-Executive Directors to join their board; 1 Co-Chair and 2 Non-Executive Directors.

The application deadline for this role is 20 July at midnight, 2023.

Friendship Bench

Independent Non-Executive Directors


Industry: Mental Health

Friendship Bench is a globally recognised organisation that aims to enhance mental health and well-being. They break down barriers to mental health services and strive to ensure that individuals from all backgrounds can access the care they need. Their flagship initiative involves physical spaces called Friendship Bench, strategically placed in communities where trained listeners, known as "community grandmothers" or "friendship bench therapists," engage in compassionate conversations with those seeking emotional support. This approach combines evidence-based cognitive-behavioural therapy principles with local cultural contexts to provide culturally appropriate care.

In addition to their physical spaces, Friendship Bench focuses on training and capacity building to empower individuals and communities as mental health champions. They offer training programs for community members, health workers, and volunteers to provide basic mental health support and recognise signs of distress. Embracing technology and innovation, the organisation also develops mobile applications, online platforms, and telehealth solutions to extend its reach and ensure remote access to mental health resources.

Friendship Bench is searching for 3 (three) Independent Non-Executive Directors to join their board.

The application deadline for this role is 07 July at midnight, 2023.

International Finance Corporation

Non-Executive Director


Industry: Financial Services

International Finance Corporation (IFC), a sister organisation of the World Bank and a member of the World Bank Group, is the largest global development institution focused on the private sector in developing countries. The Bank Group has set two goals for the world to achieve by 2030: end extreme poverty and promote shared prosperity in every country. IFC offers investment, advisory, and asset management services to encourage private-sector development in less developed countries.

IFC nominates directors to serve on the board of client companies. IFC is seeking to nominate 1 (one) Non-Executive Director to join the board of an agribusiness institution (holding company) with operations in Ivory Coast and Burkina Faso.

The application deadline for this role is 08 July at midnight, 2023.

Fastizers Food and Confectionery Limited

Independent Non-Executive Director


Industry: Food Manufacturing

Fastizers Food and Confectionery Limited (FFCL) is a rapidly growing food manufacturing company duly incorporated in Nigeria and endorsed by the National Agency for Food and Drugs Administration and Control (NAFDAC). They opened for business in 2010 and offer packaged snacks to consumers across various platforms. Fun Cookies, their flagship product, has become a household name in the Nigerian snacks landscape, popular amongst customers of all ages. Other brands include Nibit Mini Snacks - Milk & Chocolate cookies and Bitty Cookies, which have been largely successful in the market.

FFCL has become widely accepted amongst major industry distributors and wholesalers due to the popularity and acceptance of its products nationwide. Their current distribution network covers 31 states with over 100 distributors nationwide.

Aruwa Capital Management, a growth equity investor in West Africa, has recently provided the FFCL with expansion capital to embark on the next phase of the company’s growth and to fulfil its mission of becoming a leading player in Africa's attractive food and snacks space.

Fastizers Food and Confectionery Limited is searching for 1 (one) Non-Executive Director to join their board.

The application deadline for this role is 13 July at midnight, 2023.
