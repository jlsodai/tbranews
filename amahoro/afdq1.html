The Road to AFD 2026

Announcement

The fourth edition of the Africa Forum on Displacement will be held in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia, from 15 to 18 November 2026.

Convened by Amahoro Coalition, Inkomoko, and UNHCR, in partnership with the Mastercard Foundation, the Forum brings together leaders across business, government, forced displacement, and development communities to shape how we create market-based solutions to Africa’s displacement crisis.

This year’s theme, The Next Act: Scaling Africa’s Displacement Economy, reflects a shift in focus from enterprise-level, fragmented, market-based interventions to coordinated systems that operate at scale.

Updates on Past AFD Commitments
 

Amahoro Coalition

Creating 200,000 Jobs for IDPs in Nigeria’s Agriculture Sector

Nigeria is facing one of Africa’s most severe and prolonged displacement crises, with over 3.7 million internally displaced persons (IDPs). The drivers are layered: conflict, insecurity, and increasingly frequent climate shocks. At the 2025 Forum, the Bank of Agriculture (BOA) in Nigeria made a commitment to provide financing and other farm inputs to farmers - impacting over 30,000 individuals. Post-Forum and with further engagement, the target of this partnership has been expanded to impact 200,000 individuals across Nigeria over the next three years. Following the signing of the MoU between BoA, Amahoro and BOA have made significant progress in operationalising the commitment to finance IDPs through BOA's Renewed Hope Smallholder Support and Value-Chain Fund - an aggregator-lending model that channels capital to smallholder farmers through established agricultural value chains.

The initiative was piloted in early 2026 in Northern Nigeria, with 1200 IDP farmers undergoing training and will be provided with financing and farm inputs. The next phase of financing will kick off in the coming weeks with the goal of scaling the number of IDP farmers directly enrolled and supported through the program.

 
 

Amahoro Coalition

How Somalia is Creating Jobs for FDPs through Private-Sector Partnerships

At the last Africa Forum on Displacement (AFD), Amahoro Coalition mobilized partners from Somalia in the telecommunications, energy, and education sectors to discuss ways to build meaningful collaborations. At the end of our Somalia side event at AFD, our partners made commitments to create 15,000 jobs for displaced people. Over the last 12 months, the Amahoro Coalition has worked with our partners in Somalia to actualise 12% of the commitments into placements with 1800 individuals placed in jobs.

Examples of implementation include our work with Benadir Technical Institute (BTI), which has made a commitment to expand pathways to employment for forcibly displaced people. Faced with a shortage of skilled technicians, BECO (the largest utility providing power to 17 districts in the Banadir region) partnered with BTI, with the support of Amahoro Coalition, to build a pipeline of job-ready talent through demand-driven training linked directly to employment.

The success of the BTI-BECO partnership, with over 500 trainees placed in jobs, has created a ripple effect. Recognizing the power of this model, the Benadir Water Association, a coalition of 56 water companies, is looking to replicate the model to solve its own critical shortage of skilled plumbers. In addition to this, Amahoro Coalition has signed an MOU to serve as a technical advisor on IDP economic integration under a Ministry of Interior mandate — embedding private sector-led refugee inclusion within national economic systems.

Inkomoko

Dadaab's $136M Market: The Case for Private Sector Investment

Following their commitment at the 2025 Forum, Inkomoko though a market study of Dadaab - conducted in collaboration with TechnoServe and the Swiss Agency for Development and Cooperation - responds directly with actionable evidence that Dadaab is an established and functioning market, with approximately 15,000 businesses and generating an estimated US$136 million in annual activity.

Priority investment areas are clear: energy, access to finance, infrastructure, and mobility. Restrictions, high costs, and slow growth in these areas continue to suppress enterprise performance. Targeted investment in solar energy, solar refrigeration, working capital finance, and connectivity hardware can unlock immediate productivity gains, with the potential to increase MSME incomes by 20–40% annually.

As preparations advance for the next Africa Forum on Displacement, the Dadaab study provides a practical roadmap for investment, underscoring the need to translate evidence into market growth.

UNHCR

Partnership between UNHCR and Nigeria's Tropical General Investments (TGI)

Rahila Joseph, an IDP from Chibok, tending and harvesting vegetables in a community farm in Nigeria under the humanitarian livelihood programs that teach farming skills, support food production, and promote self-reliance among displaced communities.

Photo credit: Uchenna Okoro

Following the Forum in 2025, UNHCR signed an MoU with Tropical General Investments (TGI) to work together over the next 5 years to advance socio-economic inclusion and co-create sustainable solutions for forcibly displaced people in Nigeria.

The partnership leverages UNHCR’s protection and livelihoods expertise and TGI’s large‑scale agribusiness and value‑chain experience to create market‑based livelihood opportunities for forcibly displaced people and their host communities in Nigeria.

TGI, which pledged to support 5,000 displaced persons in Northwest Nigeria through agricultural value chains, is now active in Katsina, Benue, and Cross River States — currently reaching around 5,600 households (or 28,000 individuals) with activities including value chain integration, land recovery, agricultural inputs, skills development, employment, and financial inclusion.
