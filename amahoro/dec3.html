December 2025



Thank you to our clients, partners and friends for your continued engagement and shared commitment to expanding economic opportunity for forcibly displaced persons (FDPs) and host communities alike across Africa. What is increasingly clear is that the question before us is no longer whether FDP’s access to economic opportunity can be scaled, but how quickly we are prepared to turn intention into execution.

Across our collective work, we see a growing alignment. Governments are demonstrating what enabling policy looks like in practice. The private sector is showing that inclusion is not charity, but sound economics. And people with lived experience continue to remind us that dignity comes from the opportunity to work, contribute and shape one’s own future.

This reality is reinforced by evidence. This year, we launched the Pathways to Employment report series across 15 countries, which underscores the need to move beyond pilots toward systems. Systems that align policy with labour-market demand, recognise skills, enable mobility, and are financed as long-term investments in shared prosperity.

At Amahoro Coalition, we believe that employment is the most durable solution available to protracted displacement crises. When FDPs work, businesses grow, labour shortages are addressed, and societies become more resilient. This is responsibility-sharing in its most practical form.

Progress, however, depends on deeper partnerships and sharper focus. That is why we prioritise sectors with real job-creation potential, such as agriculture, and why we are proud to partner with the Bank of Agriculture of Nigeria to support the creation of over 200,000 jobs for refugees and internally displaced people. This commitment is about demonstrating what is possible and inspiring others to move faster and do more.

As we look ahead, we want to turn commitments into measurable outcomes, scale what works across borders and sectors and ensure that inclusion is built into the systems that shape our economies.

We look forward to continuing this journey with you and to deepening this conversation at the fourth Africa Forum on Displacement that will take place next year. Thank you for your partnership, your leadership, and your shared belief that inclusive labour markets are not only possible, but essential.

Wishing you and your loved ones a joyful festive season, and looking ahead to 2026 with renewed ambition, to do more and to do it together!

From all of us at Amahoro Coalition

Our final and most exciting note of the 2025: Join us on the Peak to Purpose Journey



As we close out the year, we are excited to share one final invitation with clients, partners and friends, and it is one that quite literally takes us to new heights.

In February 2026, a group of changemakers from across Africa and the Middle East will summit Mount Kilimanjaro as part of Peak to Purpose: From Kilimanjaro to Kakuma, an initiative by Amahoro Coalition. The climb supports the creation of a vibrant co-working hub and café in the Kakuma Refugee Camp, designed to spark collaboration, creativity and opportunity for entrepreneurs, youth, and the wider community.

We invite you to be part of this powerful journey. Whether by sharing the story, contributing to the initiative or joining the climb itself, every step helps build a platform where potential can thrive.
