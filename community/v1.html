Reputation vs Readiness

Many boards still equate reputation with readiness, valuing prestige, titles, and visibility over proven capability. This article challenges that mindset, arguing that effective leadership depends on substance, not status. It calls for a shift from optics to outcomes: prioritising contextual awareness, consistent delivery, and the ability to elevate teams over the allure of a well-known name.

The Shame Economy: How women pay to stay respectable

We call it “protecting your image,” “keeping the peace,” or “not letting them drag you.” But beneath those phrases lies a powerful form of social control, one that costs women money, visibility, credibility, and sometimes legacy itself.
