Amahoro Coalition and Etihad launch school and Skills Hub for urban refugee families in Nairobi


In Nairobi, many urban refugees live outside the structured support of camps yet remain excluded from formal urban systems, leaving education and livelihoods, particularly for women and children, fragile and uncertain. In response, Amahoro Coalition and Etihad Airways have launched the Abu Dhabi Community Development Centre, a school and integrated training hub managed by the refugee-led CODE Society, providing early childhood and primary education alongside vocational and entrepreneurship pathways for women. By investing in permanent community infrastructure rather than short term aid, the centre will help children remain in safe learning environments while enabling mothers to secure stable income opportunities, strengthening household resilience and advancing long term economic inclusion.

Amahoro Fellow Mo Harawe premieres new film at IFFR 2026 in Rotterdam



Last month, Whispers of a Burning Scent, a short film by Somali filmmaker and Amahoro Fellow Mo Harawe, premiered at the 55th edition of the International Film Festival Rotterdam (IFFR) as part of the inaugural slate of the Displacement Film Fund. The premiere was hosted by actor and producer Cate Blanchett, Global Goodwill Ambassador for UNHCR, alongside IFFR’s Hubert Bals Fund. Mo’s film is one of five projects awarded a €100,000 grant through the Displacement Film Fund, which supports displaced filmmakers and those telling authentic stories of displacement, alongside selected filmmakers from Ukraine, Syria, Iran, and Afghanistan. Amahoro Coalition is proud to be a founding partner of the Displacement Film Fund together with MASTER MIND, UNIQLO, Droom en Daad and the Tamer Family Foundation, with the fund managed by the Hubert Bals Fund and UNHCR serving as Strategic Partner.

Reflecting on the premiere, Isaac Kwaku Fokuo, Curator at Amahoro Coalition, asked fellow founding partners of the Displacement Film Fund to share why they chose to support the initiative. Here’s what they said:


At MASTER MIND, every engaged project should be rooted in a commitment to addressing social issues. The Displacement Film Fund was established by directly confronting one of the most urgent global issues of our time and MASTER MIND chose to participate as a funding partner.

— Koji Yanai, MASTER MIND


By initiating the DFF, it has opened a vital new door towards discovering and empowering new talent charged with a genuine message. I believe the world is full of talent; those who can afford to voice themselves and make a difference and those who never had the opportunity in the first place.

— Ayman Tamer, Tamer Family Foundation


Having continued refugee support for more than 20 years, one thing is clear to us: the refugee crisis is a global challenge of such magnitude that it cannot be solved by any single company alone. Addressing this challenge requires new ways of thinking—and partners who are willing to work together toward solutions.

— UNIQLO


Through DFF, creating space for filmmakers disrupts indifference, challenges misinformation and dismantles the idea that displacement is someone else’s problem. Supporting displaced artists matters because film reaches people through story and shared humanity.

— Aarti Lohia, SP Lohia Foundation

Amahoro Coalition team and partners summit Kilimanjaro to fund Kakuma Co-Working Centre


Last month, a team of seven climbers from Kenya, South Africa and Dubai, including members of the Amahoro team, successfully summited Mount Kilimanjaro as part of the Peak to Purpose: From Kilimanjaro to Kakuma campaign. The six day expedition to Africa’s highest peak was undertaken to raise $250,000 for a co working resource centre in Kakuma Refugee Camp, a space that will support digital learning, entrepreneurship and professional development for displaced and host community youth. Facing freezing temperatures, thin air and a midnight ascent to Uhuru Peak, the team described the climb as a reminder that progress is collective rather than individual, mirroring the resilience shown daily in Kakuma. With the summit completed, fundraising now continues toward turning the symbolic achievement into lasting infrastructure that expands opportunity and livelihoods in the camp, alongside an upcoming documentary and impact updates tracking the centre’s development.
