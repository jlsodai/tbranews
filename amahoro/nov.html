The Amahoro Skills Hub Roadshow goes to Rwanda



We visited the African Leadership University and Kepler College in Kigali, Rwanda to introduce our new Skills Hub which is Amahoro Coalition’s digital platform designed to connect forcibly displaced persons to meaningful economic opportunities across Africa. The Hub features job and internship opportunities from across the continent with a focus on refugees and displaced talent, enables partners to create accounts and upload their talent pools, generates analytics to measure impact, allows direct referrals to employers, and helps identify companies and sectors ready to absorb skilled candidates. Students from Rwanda and across Africa explored how the platform links young people to practical training, mentorship, and employment opportunities aligned with their ambitions. The conversations also highlighted entrepreneurship as a route to self-reliance and offered guidance on crafting a strong, well-rounded CV, followed by an engaging Q&A.

Our media partner African Business has published a feature titled "How a shift of viewpoint can see refugees as assets and innovators," reflecting on our side event held during UNGA 80. The article highlights a core message from the discussion: while Africa hosts 50 million displaced people, often confined to overcrowded camps with limited prospects, a change in perspective reveals refugee communities as economic contributors and hubs of innovation. With conflicts and climate pressures driving further displacement, the piece underscores the need for solutions that move beyond food and shelter toward models that unlock talent, enterprise, and resilience. Rather than being seen as burdens, refugees can become active participants in shaping economies, and settlements can serve as environments where ideas and innovation benefit society at large.

Unlocking the Potential of Refugee Markets in Africa



Our colleagues Gerald Kilimo, Saniya Syeda and Kris Senani reflect on how to unlock the potential of refugee markets in Africa, drawing parallels with Kenya’s financial sector transformation. They recall how banks once dismissed small farmers and informal traders as "unproductive," until Equity Bank removed barriers, redesigned products and proved that inclusion could be both profitable and transformative. They also argue that refugee economies sit at a similar turning point, too often framed as charity despite evidence of strong, responsive markets, and poses a compelling challenge: if Kenya’s informal economy was once misunderstood and then unlocked through design, trust, and scale, what are we still getting wrong about refugee markets today?

Next month in Geneva, the second Global Refugee Forum Progress Review will convene to accelerate the matching of pledges with resources and partners, while mobilizing renewed support for policies that advance protection, inclusion, and durable solutions. As a stakeholder to the Global Refugee Compact, Amahoro Coalition will take part in the Review, reflecting on the commitments we made at the 2019 and 2023 Forums to mobilize the private sector in creating market-based opportunities for refugees. Our pledges include securing 50,000 jobs for forcibly displaced individuals, catalyzing over USD 30 million in private-sector investment benefiting displaced communities and hosting markets, and supporting 120 displaced youth leaders across Africa through training, mentorship, private-sector connections, and market access via our Fellowship program. We will also disburse more than USD 5 million to help these young leaders grow and scale their organizations.
