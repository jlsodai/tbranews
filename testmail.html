Relevant Industry

AgriTech

Farmerline launched in 2013, with operations serving 800 farmers across Ghana – and a massive ambition to change a whole sector. Imagine Worldwide

Application Deadline

3rd October, 2022

Relevant Industry

Sector Agnostic

Imagine Worldwide (IWW) is empowering children around the globe to build literacy and numeracy skills to achieve their full potential. Literate and numerate children have better education, health and employment outcomes. These improvements are multigenerational, extending beyond an individual child to a whole family and future generations. Imagine Worldwide serves children who fall in one of these categories: in-school but without access to quality education, out-of-school, and refugees.


Their child-directed, technology-enabled solution to literacy and numeracy is massively scalable, evidence-based and enables children to drive their learning at their own pace, using software that provides a complete, research-based curriculum and pedagogy. The software is delivered to the learner on a tablet, does not require connectivity, and is charged by solar power or other appropriate energy sources. While all instruction is provided via the tablet, adults play a critical role in the model, lending support, coaching and mentoring. They do not, however, need to be highly trained teachers.


Imagine Worldwide brings training, tools, and expertise from prior implementations to co-create an implementation model with partners that address their specific needs and context. In this manner, they have successfully implemented the model in a wide range of contexts, such as government schools, refugee camps and home-based settings, and with a wide range of implementation partners.


The organisation has decided to focus exclusively on Sub-Saharan Africa for the next few years due to enormous demand, a lack of comparable organisations, a strong fit for the learning solutions, and operational benefits derived from clustering. At the moment, Imagine Worldwide operates in the following African countries: Tanzania, Malawi, Sierra Leone, Liberia, Burkina Faso, Senegal and Ghana.


To support its expansion efforts, as they will be serving millions of children over the next 5 years, Imagine Worldwide is searching for 2 (two) Non-Executive Directors to join its board of directors.


The application deadline for this role is 3rd October at midnight, 2022.

Growth Investment Partners Ghana

Application Deadline

22nd September 2022

Relevant Industry

Investment, Fundraising

British International Investment (BII) is the UK Government's Development Finance Institution, with a mission to help solve the biggest global development challenges by investing patient, flexible capital to support private-sector growth and innovation. They have investments in almost 1200 businesses in emerging economies with total net assets of £6.8bn and a portfolio of £5.2bn. BII is an investor with two objectives: to support the business growth and economic stability that will enable countries to leave poverty behind and to make a financial return, which is reinvested to improve the lives of millions of people in Africa and South Asia. Like other Development Finance Institutions (DFIs), they aim to increase capital flows to underdeveloped markets so countries can finance their own way out of poverty.


In Ghana, MSMEs (micro, small and medium enterprises) contribute 70% to GDP, 80% to employment, and makeup 90% of all enterprises in the country. They play a critical role in creating job opportunities, providing products and services, and improving economic dynamism and competition. However, access to long-term finance is a key constraint to the growth of such businesses. When looking at SMEs, most rely on internally generated cash to fund expansion projects. Only half have access to credit from banks (75% of which is shorter than 3 years in tenor), and very few have raised Private Equity or Venture Capital financing.


BII, together with an experienced Ghanaian management team - is creating a solution to address this persistent development issue and capitalise on the large untapped commercial opportunity by establishing a new and innovative investment company: Growth Investment Partners Ghana. The aim is to sustainably deploy long-term growth capital into under-funded small to mid-cap businesses in Ghana. The company will be regulated by Ghana's Securities Exchange Commission and will look to raise funding from local institutions (e.g., Pension Funds as an entity regulated by the National Pension Fund Regulatory Authority). Under the business plan, Growth Investment Partners Ghana intends to invest approximately US$240 million (in Ghana cedi equivalent) in 130+ small to mid-cap businesses across various sectors of the economy over the next 15 years.


Growth Investment Partners Ghana is currently building its Investment Committee, whereby the responsibility for the investment process will be delegated. The IC will be composed of up to 3 Independent members and 2 members of the management team.


BII is searching for 3 (three) Investment Committee Members to join the Investment Committee of Growth Investment Partners Ghana.


The application deadline for this role is 22nd September at midnight, 2022.
