Marjorie Ngwenya da Silva’s Board Journey

Marjorie Ngwenya da Silva

External Member, Prudential Regulation Committee, Bank of England

Transitioning from executive management to board leadership came earlier than I anticipated. By openly sharing my aspirations and leveraging my network, I was able to secure valuable opportunities and build a diverse board portfolio across different sectors and regions.

We are delighted to continue our Board Journeys series, where we delve into the unique pathways that have led our members to impactful board positions. In this feature, we spotlight Marjorie Ngwenya, a seasoned actuary and former President of the Institute and Faculty of Actuaries (IFoA), who brings a wealth of knowledge and experience to her board roles.

Meet a new member: Alfred Olajide

Vice President, East and Central Africa at Coca-Cola Africa

Following the enthusiastic reception of our male membership launch, we continue to welcome outstanding leaders into our community.

This month, we are pleased to introduce Alfred Olajide, Vice President for East and Central Africa at Coca-Cola Africa. Alfred shares his motivation for joining TheBoardroom Africa:

“I believe opening up the community to male members further enriches diversity of thought, encourages allyship and a sense of community.”

“I was impressed by the vision of TBrA and found it a good platform for me to connect with like minds, and contribute to the growth of any suitable organisation as a board member. I was also attracted to its networking opportunities and leadership development programmes.

I believe opening up the community to male members further enriches diversity of thought, encourages allyship and a sense of community. A platform like this is a perfect opportunity to add value and contribute towards a diverse professional world, tapping into the collective genius, agnostic of gender.”

Interesting Reads


High stakes, strong wills, and increasing uncertainty can make decisions at the top of your organisation fraught. The backing of a board can mobilise an organisation, but a significant divergence of vision and values may lead the board to stall progress on an organisation’s highest priorities — and even unseat a CEO. Executives must proactively and productively make covert disagreements overt and foster a healthy dialogue with board members.
