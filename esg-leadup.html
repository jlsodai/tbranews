Strengthening our Mission by Ensuring Men are Part of the Solution

To our stakeholders,


Since its inception, TheBoardroom Africa (TBrA) has been dedicated to advocating for women's representation on boards and other leadership positions, driven by the stark disparity between men and women at the top. Over the past eight years, we have been unwavering in our advocacy for the inclusion of women in key leadership positions.


By remaining adaptable, we have developed a sustainable model that aligns with our mission. This commitment has led to a transformative agenda, directly impacting the leadership journeys of thousands of women. We have collaborated with regional and global businesses and organisations to reshape their leadership spaces by placing over 200 women on boards, investment committees, and in other leadership roles.


Designed to help leaders navigate the complex challenges of today's rapidly changing world, our executive certificate programmes, developed by leaders for leaders, with contextual and practical solutions, have equipped more than 600 women leaders with the knowledge and skills necessary to transform their approaches to governance and sustainability. We’ve done this all while also providing our members with a supportive and vibrant community of fellow leaders with whom they can collaborate.


We believe the inclusion of men in our community will make our impact more transformational. We also see this as a way to engage men broadly around conversations of inclusion and diversity - and truly make them part of the solution.

Today, I am writing to share an important and exciting development in our ongoing mission to foster inclusion in leadership. In our efforts to find the best, most effective ways to elevate the status of women leaders, we are continuously listening, learning and unlearning to stay relevant to our mission.


We have come to the realisation that in order to make leadership teams diverse, we need to be equally intentional and ensure that all leaders, regardless of gender, are provided with the right tools and that our mission to enhance leadership opportunities for women is made richer in discourse and networks that are also diverse. We recognise that for women to advance in leadership, they need to be part of a supportive community and expand their access to men who hold the majority of leadership positions.


To that effect, we are excited to announce that TheBoardroom Africa will be opening its membership to male candidates. We believe the inclusion of men in our community will make our impact more transformational, and we also see this as a way to engage men broadly around conversations of inclusion and diversity - and truly make them part of the solution.


This change is motivated by:


  • Our belief that expanding our network by including influential male leaders will provide women members with richer expert networking opportunities, facilitating greater access to opportunity, collaboration, and learning.
  • Our desire to hone in on diversity more broadly, to include diversity in age, ethnicity, and more, aligns with our belief that varied perspectives enrich leadership.
  • Our vision for a collaborative society and our belief that where men and women work side by side, society as a whole thrives. This change amplifies our vision for harmonious and effective leadership.

While we are opening our doors to male members, our core commitment to addressing gender diversity remains steadfast. Women will continue to be the cornerstone of our community, embodying our unwavering commitment to challenge and change the gender narratives in leadership across Africa. Alongside these changes, we plan to provide enhanced services and programmes, including a broader talent base for executive search clients and richer learning experiences that benefit from wider perspectives.


We understand that change can bring about questions. We have prepared this Q&A to answer some of them. If there are questions we have not addressed, you may email them to: info@theboardroomafrica.com


Thank you for your continued commitment to TheBoardroom Africa. Your support and dedication have been instrumental in our success so far. We look forward to continuing this journey together, breaking barriers and paving the way for a future where authentic diversity is ingrained in the fabric of executive leadership.


Warm regards

Founder & CEO
