Charity Updates

 

Spring is in full swing here in London 🌸. We’ve just closed applications for the September 2026 round, and our February cohort incubatees are knee-deep in final proposal writing. We’re excited to share a fresh set of updates and opportunities from across the AIM community!

In this issue

Charity Updates

▶ News & Milestones

01

Giving Green

Giving Green recently published a new strategy report on Reducing Biodiversity Loss and a list of Top Biodiversity Nonprofits. The new research is the result of a consulting project supported by an anonymous donor. Addressing climate change remains at the heart and soul of Giving Green’s mission, and 100% of donations to the Giving Green Fund will continue to fund climate solutions.

 

02

Vida Plena

2,500 people marks a meaningful milestone for Vida Plena, reflecting the number of participants who have taken part in our group-based mental wellbeing programs since our founding in 2022. What began as a small pilot of just 60 participants has grown into a community-driven model reaching individuals across Ecuador — with each person influencing an average of 4 family members.

 
 

03

Opal Health

Opal Health completes program design phase in partnership with the Ugandan Government!

Five months in, we have finished our second trip to Uganda and completed the design phase. The Ugandan Ministry of Health and District government have helped us develop a program honed to be as impactful as possible in the eastern region of Uganda. All we need to do now is launch next month — then iterate and expand through the east. Get in touch to hear more or support us.

 
 
 

04

High Impact Professionals

High Impact Professionals just kicked off the latest round of their Impact Accelerator Program, with a record-breaking 100 participants. So far in 2026, HIP’s alumni have completed nine job transitions into high-impact roles and two board appointments at LEEP and CEA, as well as founding new organisations under AIM’s Founding to Give program.

 

05

Healthy Futures Global

Nils is in Angola for the launch of our pilot there — working with the Ministry of Health to test 1,000 pregnant women for syphilis, and treat them if needed. If successful, the pilot will pave the way to reach 1.3 million pregnant women in Angola each year. Keyur was also interviewed by Coefficient Giving about what led him to leave working as a doctor and co-found Healthy Futures through AIM’s Charity Entrepreneurship program.

 
 
 

06

CEARCH

CEARCH raised a further USD 50,000 for high-impact health policy projects in Q1. In 2025, CEARCH prioritised engaging non-EA philanthropists in the Asia-Pacific to promote effective giving. While the environment is challenging, there have been some concrete successes as well as a number of promising leads, and CEARCH intends to continue working on this area in 2026.

 

07

Notify Health

Co-Founder Sam Harvey has stepped away from an operational role to serve as a trustee and board member. Co-Founder Daniel Handschuh will step into the CEO role, leading Notify Health’s continued growth with a strengthened team including two new members: Bema Boateng (Program Innovation Associate) and Oluwafunmito Odefemi (MEL Manager).

 
 

08

NOVAH (No Violence At Home)

Season 2 of Twubakane officially began airing in Rwanda, launching the intervention phase of NOVAH’s randomised controlled trial.

Over recent months, NOVAH distributed 2,400 radios, enrolled 4,800 participants across 96 villages, and trained 192 youth volunteers. For the next 3 months, couples in treatment villages tune in to Radio Nyagatare at 8:15 pm — right after the evening news when listenership peaks — for approximately 20-minute episodes.

 
 

09

Fish Welfare Initiative

FWI recently shared:

‣ A new continuous water quality dataset
‣ Farm program updates: M&E report and stocking density impact changes

FWI staff at their research facility observing fish in their ongoing feed fortification efficacy trial.
 
 

10

Happier Lives Institute

The Happier Lives Institute has completed the first disbursements from the Happier Lives Fund, directing donations to its recommended charities. Launched in December 2025, the Fund raised $72,585.57 in just one month. Read this blog post to find out how the money was allocated.

With the World Happiness Report 2026 out on 19 March, HLI shared their favourite findings. Founder Michael Plant also published his reflections one year after writing a World Happiness Report chapter in 2025.

 
 

11

Spiro

Spiro has been invited by the National TB program of Pakistan to contribute to the next National Strategic Plan formulation and subsequent Global Fund grant development process, anticipated throughout April — an important juncture every 3 years that sets country priorities on TB.

We also hosted a webinar for World TB Day showcasing our results in Sindh province, onboarded Aesha Haider as a new Founder’s Associate, and have been carrying out Q1 program reviews plus Q2 planning.
