Consolidating influence in any
boardroom: Tokunboh Ishmael’s journey

to Chartered Director

Completing the journey towards achieving the Charted Director qualification, even after sitting on various boards and committees for a number of years, has been valuable. It has strengthened my conviction on the necessary processes and value of proper governance and provided additional language and frameworks to enable me to be an effective board director and chair.

I had a sense that challenging the management of our portfolio companies on governance matters was an essential part of my role as a director, but I had much pushback from management and some fellow directors. In one case, I had to go up against the chair of a board and fellow directors to ensure proper processes for governance and decision-making were followed.

It was lonely and hard. For this reason, professional development and to maintain my sanity on difficult boards, I decided to pursue the CDir, going beyond the initial certificate through Open Doors and the Diploma in Company Direction.

About Open Doors

Curated in partnership with the Institute of Directors UK (IoD UK), the Open Doors programme offers an intensive learning experience led by expert course lecturers. With a focus on governance, finance, leadership and strategy, it equips aspiring and current directors with the essential skills for effective leadership in your organisations and on boards.
