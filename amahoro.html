Across Africa, millions of refugees and displaced people, many of them young and ready to contribute, face significant barriers to employment. Although national policies often allow refugees to work, complex procedures, unclear regulations, and limited support make access to jobs extremely difficult. Employers who want to hire refugees also face legal uncertainties and bureaucratic challenges.



To address the gap between policy and practice, Amahoro Coalition, in partnership with the Mastercard Foundation, has launched the Pathways to Employment series. This initiative examines refugee employment across 15 African countries, exploring legal frameworks, administrative processes, employer challenges, and ways to strengthen collaboration.

The series is part of a broader effort to connect Africa’s growing youth population, including those from displaced communities, with inclusive and dignified work opportunities. With a strong focus on women and young people, the reports aim to guide better policies and inspire new partnerships between the private sector and refugee talent.

Amahoro Coalition joins high-level roundtable in Bangui to advance private sector solutions for displacement in Central Africa



As core members of the Central African Republic Solutions Support Platform (CAR-SSP), Amahoro Coalition joined international delegations in Bangui for the High-Level Private Sector Roundtable on 1 and 2 July. Hosted by President Faustin-Archange Touadéra and UNHCR, the event brought together ministries, business leaders, and development partners from CAR, Cameroon, Chad, DRC, and the Republic of Congo to explore how the private sector can support solutions for returnees and displaced people.

We shared insights from our Fellowship program, which highlights refugee talent and entrepreneurship. We also shared our work in displaced communities around improving business conditions, expanding access to finance, addressing skills gaps and investing in sectors like agriculture and infrastructure. We also engaged with partners including the World Bank, African Development Bank, and several companies interested in future collaboration. The forum ended with a clear call to develop a roadmap, establish a monitoring committee, and sustain public-private dialogue to deliver lasting impact.

Amahoro Coalition and Zeleman Academy join forces to unlock refugee talent in Africa’s creative economy



Amahoro Coalition and Zeleman Communications are exploring a new partnership aimed at expanding access to meaningful creative opportunities for refugee and displaced youth across Africa. At the heart of this collaboration is the upcoming launch of the Zeleman Academy, whose inaugural cohort will include refugee candidates eager to contribute their skills and perspectives to the continent’s growing creative industry.

The partnership builds on conversations that began during the last Africa Forum on Displacement, where leaders, creatives, and private sector actors came together to reimagine how displaced communities can be more fully included in Africa’s development story. Among the Forum’s participants was Zelalem Woldemariam, Founder and CEO of Zeleman Communications, who sees this next step as a natural continuation of that vision.

“At the Forum, we saw the creativity, talent, and potential of displaced youth. This partnership is about moving from ideas to action, giving that talent the space to grow, create, and shape the future of African storytelling,” said Zelalem.

As the Academy prepares to open its doors, this collaboration reflects a shared belief: that refugee youth have not only a right to work, but a vital role to play in shaping Africa’s cultural and economic future. By investing in their creative potential, we take one step closer to building a continent where inclusion fuels innovation and where every story has the power to inspire change.

Amahoro Fellows meet in Abuja


Amahoro West Africa Fellows recently came together in Abuja for an intensive three-day program focused on leadership, branding, and network building. The Fellows, representing diverse ventures across the region, engaged in masterclasses, strategic collaboration, and even a hands-on cooking challenge designed to test leadership under pressure. With expert guidance from award-winning presenter Zahirah Marty, Fellows refined their brand stories, clarified their visions, and crafted strategies to amplify their impact. From personal storytelling to kitchen teamwork, the intensive offered powerful lessons in resilience, clarity, and collaboration.
