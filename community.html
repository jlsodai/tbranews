5th Edition of Ghana Board Diversity Index (BDI) Report Launched

TheBoardroom Africa (TBrA), in collaboration with the Ghana Stock Exchange (GSE), is proud to present the 5th Edition of the Ghana Board Diversity Index Report. This comprehensive report provides an in-depth analysis of the current state of gender diversity on the boards of Ghana’s listed companies, highlighting both strides and setbacks in the journey towards gender parity.

While women's leadership in executive roles has increased, the report shows a decline in their overall representation on boards, indicating mixed progress in advancing gender diversity in Ghana’s corporate leadership landscape.

Consultant Neurosurgeon at Lucca Medical Health Centre

2024 Medical Industry Trends


Dr Teddy Totimeh, a leading neurosurgeon in Ghana, shares his insights on the future of medical specialty development, spotlighting trends shaping the healthcare landscape. He highlights the growing impact of private medical entrepreneurship, particularly in tackling diseases affecting Ghanaians in their most productive years.

Dr Totimeh believes that small, agile surgical centers will play a pivotal role in advancing specialised care, with an emphasis on attracting philanthropic support to reduce financial pressures and enhance training. However, he stresses that financing remains the biggest challenge, calling for banks to collaborate with specialists to create multidisciplinary groups that can transform healthcare into a value-generating business. Explore Dr Totimeh’s perspective on how these trends are shaping the future of medical care in Ghana.

Securing Executive Buy-In for ESG: Expert Insights from Christine Uri

Christine Uri, a Top 100 Voice in Sustainability and member of our ESG Certificate Programme faculty, offers practical strategies for gaining executive support for ESG initiatives in her article "How To Build Executive Support For ESG" (published on Above the Law, May 2024).

She outlines the importance of identifying leadership supporters and detractors, addressing concerns like costs or public perception with data, and educating executives on ESG trends. By showcasing benefits in sales, compliance, and talent retention, Christine demonstrates how cross-functional support can drive ESG progress.

Christine will join Rosalind Kainyah, Alison Taylor, Janhavi Dadarkar and Marjorie Ngwenya da Silva to deliver next year’s ESG Certificate Programme cohorts for TheBoardroom Africa.
