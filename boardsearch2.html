Farmerline

Relevant Industry

AgriTech

Farmerline launched in 2013, with operations serving 800 farmers across Ghana – and a massive ambition to change a whole sector. In a world that needs to double productivity to feed itself by 2050, Farmerline's value is clear. They enable everyone in the chain to prosper. More food, more profit, more impact. Since its inception, Farmerline's mission has been to use tech to give farmers the tools to create lasting wealth: quality inputs, fair credit, ongoing training, and market access.


Farmerline previously focused on smallholders but now works with hundreds of farmer associations and partners. They help national governments extend their knowledge, hone their techniques, and supply blue-chip manufacturers with fully traceable commodities for their increasingly demanding consumers. Farmerline's work proves that with passion, perseverance and the right partners, you can move the needle and start to change a system. At the moment, 51% of Farmerline's farmers are black women.


Farmerline recently closed a Pre-Series A round and is searching for an Independent Chairperson to join its mission to transform farming.

Imagine Worldwide

Relevant Industry

Sector Agnostic

Imagine Worldwide (IWW) is empowering children around the globe to build literacy and numeracy skills to achieve their full potential. Literate and numerate children have better education, health and employment outcomes. These improvements are multigenerational, extending beyond an individual child to a whole family and future generations. Imagine Worldwide serves children who fall in one of these categories: in-school but without access to quality education, out-of-school, and refugees.


Their child-directed, technology-enabled solution to literacy and numeracy is massively scalable, evidence-based and enables children to drive their learning at their own pace, using software that provides a complete, research-based curriculum and pedagogy. The software is delivered to the learner on a tablet, does not require connectivity, and is charged by solar power or other appropriate energy sources. While all instruction is provided via the tablet, adults play a critical role in the model, lending support, coaching and mentoring. They do not, however, need to be highly trained teachers.


Imagine Worldwide brings training, tools, and expertise from prior implementations to co-create an implementation model with partners that address their specific needs and context. In this manner, they have successfully implemented the model in a wide range of contexts, such as government schools, refugee camps and home-based settings, and with a wide range of implementation partners.


The organisation has decided to focus exclusively on Sub-Saharan Africa for the next few years due to enormous demand, a lack of comparable organisations, a strong fit for the learning solutions, and operational benefits derived from clustering. At the moment, Imagine Worldwide operates in the following African countries: Tanzania, Malawi, Sierra Leone, Liberia, Burkina Faso, Senegal and Ghana.


To support its expansion efforts, as they will be serving millions of children over the next 5 years, Imagine Worldwide is searching for 2 (two) Non-Executive Directors to join its board of directors.

CivSource Africa

Application Deadline

4th November, 2022

Relevant Industry

Philanthropy

CivSource Africa is an independent advisory organisation that seeks to refine the practice and footprint of philanthropy in Africa, for authentic civic engagement. They are pioneers in the space where philanthropy and civil society meet and seek to foster an environment that nurtures mutually beneficial relationships and stewardship of entrusted resources. To influence practices CivSource Africa identifies and supports the work of funding agencies and philanthropists in Africa, and particularly seeks to connect with and support local and Africa-led philanthropy.


CivSource Africa provides grant management and advisory services on context and strategy, next to thought leadership on strategic financing models for effective philanthropy and civil engagement. As ambassadors of stories of change, told in ways that empower and sustain flourishing communities, they also facilitate open and informed conversations pertaining to philanthropic giving and civil society organisations. It operates in Uganda, South Africa and recently in the Kenyan market.


CivSource Africa is searching for 5 (five) Non-Executive Directors to join their inaugural board.


The application deadline for this role is 4th November at midnight, 2022.
