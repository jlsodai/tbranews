Africa CEO Forum 2026

Where Does Business and Talent Go When People Are Forced to Flee?

From left to right: Isaac Kwaku Fokuo, Curator at Amahoro Coalition, Maj Gen (Rtd) Albert Murasira, Minister in charge of Refugee Affairs in Rwanda and Dr. Monique Nsanzabaganwa, former Deputy Chairperson of the African Union

Alongside the Africa CEO Forum 2026, Amahoro Coalition hosted an exclusive CEO roundtable at Kigali Golf Villas & Resort, bringing together African business leaders, policymakers, and investors to explore how displacement and human mobility can drive economic growth, innovation, and market expansion across the continent. Centered around the question "Where does talent and business go when people move?" the conversation challenged leaders to move beyond viewing displaced populations solely through a humanitarian lens and instead recognize them as entrepreneurs, workers, consumers, and contributors to Africa's economic future.

Hosted by Monique Nsanzabaganwa, former African Union Deputy Chairperson, the gathering highlighted practical opportunities for private sector engagement ranging from workforce integration and entrepreneurship support to digital jobs and financial inclusion. The roundtable concluded with a strong call for deeper collaboration between businesses, governments, and development partners to build more inclusive and resilient African economies.

In Depth

Why Private Sector-Led Economic Inclusion and Labour Mobility for Displaced Africans Matters Now

In this conversation with Isaac Kwaku Fokuo Jr., recorded alongside the Africa CEO Forum 2026, we explore why economic inclusion and labour mobility for forcibly displaced people have become one of the defining imperatives of our time. Drawing from more than half a decade of Amahoro Coalition's work across the displacement sector and the impact of its various initiatives across the continent, Isaac argues that the conversation can no longer remain confined to humanitarian response alone. As Africa faces rapid demographic growth, rising displacement, and shifting labour market realities, he makes the case for viewing displaced people as workers, entrepreneurs, consumers, and contributors to economic growth. Through stories and examples from across Africa, the discussion highlights how businesses, governments, and communities can unlock new opportunities by investing in mobility, talent, and inclusion, while helping shape a more resilient and prosperous future for the continent.

Cannes Film Festival

Amahoro Coalition and Partners Announce Five New Displacement Film Fund Laureates at Cannes Film Festival

The selected laureates are Mohammed Amer, Annemarie Jacir, Akuol de Mabior, Bao Nguyen and Rithy Panh.

Partner Highlight

From the BAL Finals in Kigali: Our Partner NABU Champions Storytelling, Literacy, and Displaced Creatives

As Kigali hosts the finals of the Basketball Africa League (BAL), NABU partnered with Basketball Africa League and Visit Rwanda to launch Rocket Rukundo's Jungle Escapade, a new children's book aimed at inspiring young readers through sport, storytelling, and local language literacy. Through NABU's network of schools, reading programs, educators, and digital platforms reaching millions of children around the world, the BAL connects with young readers at a scale and depth that goes far beyond the arena, into classrooms, community spaces, and homes across the continent.

This mission strongly aligns with our work and thus we have partnered with NABU to support displaced creatives and storytellers across the continent. Through the partnership, Sudanese writers and illustrators with lived experience of displacement have been supported to create a special series of children's books that reflect their cultures, languages, and stories. The collaboration is part of our wider work around the creative economy and how it can create both opportunity and representation for displaced communities while ensuring children see themselves reflected in the stories they read.

Workforce Innovation

Amahoro Coalition and KEPSA Convene Business Leaders on the Future of Work

Amahoro Coalition, in collaboration with the Kenya Private Sector Alliance (KEPSA), convened the Workforce Innovation for Business Growth Breakfast Meeting to explore how businesses can unlock underutilized talent pipelines and strengthen workforce resilience through inclusive hiring. Bringing together private sector leaders including HACO Industries, NABU, and Talent Pool Consulting, the conversation focused on refugee and displaced talent not as a social intervention, but as a practical solution to labour market challenges. Discussions highlighted the business case for inclusion, from improved retention and access to multilingual talent, to skills relevant for emerging digital sectors. The session also showcased the role of the Amahoro Skills Hub in connecting employers to vetted, work-ready talent across the region, reinforcing a growing recognition that the future of work in Africa will increasingly depend on diverse, cross-border talent pools and stronger private sector leadership in economic inclusion.

Project Update

Stitching New Futures for Refugee Women in Uganda

As part of our partnership with the Sheikha Fatima Fund for Refugee Women, we recently visited the Zuhur Al Amal Sewing Program in Uganda, an initiative supporting refugee and host-community women through vocational training in tailoring, textile production, entrepreneurship, and financial literacy. Implemented with iProfile Foundation, the program currently supports 20 forcibly displaced women and is set to scale to 50 participants in the next phase. Beyond skills training, the initiative connects women to broader economic opportunities through partnerships with organizations including Ecobank, DHL, and Turkish Airlines, helping participants access financial services, certification, market opportunities, and long-term pathways to economic self-reliance.
