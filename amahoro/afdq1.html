Announcement

We are proud to announce that the fourth edition of the Africa Forum on Displacement will be held in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia, from 15 to 18 November 2026.

Convened by Amahoro Coalition, Inkomoko, and UNHCR, in partnership with the Mastercard Foundation, the Forum brings together leaders across business, government, forced displacement, and development communities to shape how we create market-based solutions to Africa's displacement crisis.

This year's theme, The Next Act: Scaling Africa's Displacement Economy, reflects a shift in focus from enterprise-level, fragmented, market-based interventions to coordinated systems that operate at scale.

Amahoro Coalition

How Somalia is Creating Jobs for FDPs through Private-Sector Partnerships

At the last Africa Forum on Displacement, the Banadir Technical Institute (BTI) made a clear commitment to expand pathways to employment for forcibly displaced people. In Mogadishu, that commitment is already taking shape. Faced with a shortage of skilled technicians, BECO (the largest utility providing power to 17 districts in the Banadir region) partnered with BTI to build a pipeline of job-ready talent through demand-driven training linked directly to employment. With the support of Amahoro Coalition, many trainees, are guaranteed jobs in sectors such as solar energy, earning stable incomes and rebuilding their livelihoods.

The success of the BTI-BECO partnership, with over 500 trainees placed in jobs, has created a ripple effect. Recognizing the power of this model, the Benadir Water Association, a coalition of 56 water companies, has approached BTI to solve its own critical shortage of skilled plumbers. The institute is now expanding its curriculum to include water plumbing services, once again ensuring that its training programs are directly aligned with the urgent needs of the private sector.

Inkomoko

Dadaab's $136M Market: The Case for Private Sector Investment

At the Africa Forum on Displacement 2025, governments, donors, and private sector leaders called for stronger business cases to unlock investment in displacement-affected markets.

~$136M

Annual economic activity across ~15,000 businesses in Dadaab

Inkomoko’s Dadaab market study - conducted in collaboration with TechnoServe and the Swiss Agency for Development and Cooperation - responds directly with actionable evidence.

Dadaab is an established and functioning market, with approximately 15,000 businesses and generating an estimated US$136 million in annual activity. The challenge for growth is systemic, solvable failures in finance, infrastructure, and policy. The opportunity now is to grow that market by addressing the constraints limiting growth.

Priority investment areas are clear: energy, access to finance, infrastructure, and mobility. Restrictions, high costs, and slow growth in these areas continue to suppress enterprise performance. Targeted investment in solar energy, solar refrigeration, working capital finance, and connectivity hardware can unlock immediate productivity gains, with the potential to increase MSME incomes by 20–40% annually.

Collectively working together to deploy tailored financial products for refugee-ledbusinesses, supporting last-mile infrastructure solutions, and partnering locally withecosystem actors to de-risk entry and accelerate scale are priorities to contribute to Dadaab’s growth.

As preparations advance for the next Africa Forum on Displacement, the Dadaab study provides a practical roadmap for investment, underscoring the need to translate evidence into market growth. This kind of business insight and more are what’s on the schedule for the upcoming event.

UNHCR

Partnership between UNHCR and Nigeria's Tropical General Investments (TGI)

Rahila Joseph, an IDP from Chibok, tending and harvesting vegetables in a community farm in Nigeria under the humanitarian livelihood programs that teach farming skills, support food production, and promote self-reliance among displaced communities.

Photo credit: Uchenna Okoro

Nigeria hosts over 3.6 million IDPs and more than 100,000 refugees, many living in regions with strong agricultural potential but limited economic opportunities. This reality represents the foundation of collaboration between TGI and UNHCR, which is anchored in agricultural value chains, facilitating for displaced populations to participate as producers, workers, or entrepreneurs.

The partnership leverages UNHCR’s protection and livelihoods expertise and TGI’s large‑scale agribusiness and value‑chain experience to create market‑based livelihood opportunities for forcibly displaced people and their host communities in Nigeria.

TGI, which pledged to support 5,000 displaced persons in Northwest Nigeria through agricultural value chains, is now active in Katsina, Benue, and Cross River States — currently reaching around 5,600 households (or 28,000 individuals) with activities including value chain integration, land recovery, agricultural inputs, skills development, employment, and financial inclusion.
