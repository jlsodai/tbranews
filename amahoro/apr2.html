200 Days to the Africa Forum on Displacement

In just 200 days, the fourth edition of the Africa Forum on Displacement will convene in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia from 15 to 18 November 2026. Co-convened by Amahoro Coalition, Inkomoko, and UNHCR, in partnership with the Mastercard Foundation, the Forum will bring together leaders from business, government, and development to shape the future of market-based solutions to displacement in Africa.

Workforce & Education

Building Tomorrow's Workforce Today: Insights from Kepler College Career Fair

Research shows that today, employers are no longer hiring based on technical expertise alone. As AI continues to automate routine tasks, the most in-demand graduates are those who combine digital literacy with adaptability, critical thinking, collaboration and emotional intelligence. Being “work-ready” today means mastering human skills as much as technical ones.

Against this backdrop, Amahoro Coalition joined the 2026 Annual Career Fair organized by Kepler College in Kigali, Rwanda. Held under the theme “Building Tomorrow’s Workforce Today,” the event brought together over 30 companies and highlighted a shift, where they are looking for adaptable, problem-solving talent, while students are exploring pathways beyond traditional jobs, including entrepreneurship and innovation.

Private Sector Engagement

How Does the Private Sector Create Jobs in Fragile & Conflict-Affected Economies?

On the sidelines of the Africa CEO Forum in Kigali (14–15 May), Amahoro Coalition is convening a high-level roundtable with CEOs and industry leaders to explore one of Africa’s most urgent questions: how do we create jobs in fragile and conflict-affected economies? As displacement continues to rise across the continent, this conversation places a clear focus on the role of the private sector in unlocking employment opportunities and long-term investment in displacement settings.

This builds on a call made two years ago for businesses to invest just 1% of their profits into displacement-affected communities. Since then, the crisis in Sudan has escalated, displacing over 14 million people and reinforcing the urgency to rethink how displaced talent is absorbed into host economies. More than ever, aligning private sector demand with this untapped workforce is not just a social imperative, but an economic one.

Creative Economy

Opening Doors to Africa's Creative Economy

Join us in Addis Ababa as Amahoro Coalition partners with CRAFT Addis to unlock new pathways for young displaced creators into Africa’s growing creative industry. From 30 April to 2 May, the CRAFT Addis Festival will bring together artists, innovators, and cultural leaders from across the continent and beyond to explore how creativity can drive economic growth, community development, and technological progress.

Through this partnership, young creators with lived experience of displacement will have the opportunity to connect, collaborate, and co-create alongside some of the industry’s most exciting voices.
