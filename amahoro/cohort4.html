Fellowship Cohort 4

This World Refugee Day, we proudly welcome the Fellows of Cohort 4 to the Amahoro Fellowship. Bringing together 41 entrepreneurs and social change leaders with lived experience of displacement, this is our largest and most diverse cohort yet and a powerful reminder that when talent is given the opportunity to thrive, entire communities benefit.

This year's cohort tells a remarkable story:

Women make up 63% of the Fellowship, making this our first majority women-led cohort.

Nearly two-thirds of the Fellows are entrepreneurs building businesses, while the rest are social change leaders transforming their communities through advocacy, education, health, climate action and economic inclusion.

The Fellows come from across Africa and the Middle East and are building solutions in more than a dozen African countries: from Uganda and Rwanda to Nigeria, Egypt, Zambia, South Africa, Kenya, Ethiopia and beyond. Uganda hosts the largest share of Fellows, while the Democratic Republic of Congo is the most represented country of origin.

But perhaps what makes Cohort 4 truly exceptional is the diversity of ideas.

This year's Fellows include:

Entrepreneurs creating climate-friendly products from agricultural waste.

Agribusiness leaders improve food security using solar technology.
📚

Education innovators opening doors for millions of young people.

Fellowship Impact to Date

Amahoro Fellowship Impact to date

Amahoro Fellowship is a 12-month leadership and entrepreneurship programme designed to accelerate the growth of Africa's most promising refugee-led and displacement-focused ventures.

Through mentorship, executive coaching, business development, private-sector connections, access to investors, technical support and a continent-wide network of peers, Fellows receive the tools they need to grow ideas into lasting impact. Fellows also become part of an alumni community that continues to collaborate long after the programme ends.

The impact is already being felt across Africa.

Since its launch, Amahoro Fellows have built thriving businesses, influenced policy, created jobs and expanded opportunities for thousands of displaced people and host communities.
