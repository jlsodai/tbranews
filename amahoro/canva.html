The rules are changing. Is your career keeping up?
 
Young professionals capable of delivering results remain invisible when opportunity arrives. Employers struggle to trust signals. Conventional assumptions no longer hold. Potential stalls not because talent is absent, but because readiness is not always legible to employers.
 
The phrase "future of work" has become increasingly popular. But for Africa, with 77% of its population under 35 and the fastest-growing economies on earth, this is not a trend to just speculate about. It is a reckoning already underway, reshaping who advances and who is quietly left behind.
 
On 19 February 2026, EMERGE convenes its first public event of the year, The Future of Work: a virtual town hall a two-way discussion between talent and employers on how work, leadership, and opportunity are transforming inside organisations.
 
This is not a motivational session. It is a shared examination of what is actually changing and a determination of what professionals and institutions need to do differently in response.
 

 
Speaker
 
 
Steve Cadigan
LinkedIn's First Chief HR Officer, Future of Work Obsessed, Board Member, Teacher & Author.
 
Steve has spent decades navigating disruption, talent risk, and leadership failure from inside fast-scaling companies and is one of the most influential voices globally on the subject. He sits on TheBoardroom Africa's board and advises on the future of work across emerging markets.
 
Bring your real questions. We’ll discuss the matters that have an impact. Leave with clarity on how leadership readiness is now assessed, long before opportunities arrive.
