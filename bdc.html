As we prepare to round up 2022, we reflect on our year at the Board Diversity Charter.


We celebrate the global mandates approved in favour of gender diversity, the progress made and, most importantly, the organisations that have signed up as Signatories and Pledge Partners to the Charter in 2022.



Here’s what a few Signatories had to say about their decision to join the Charter:


“As funders, we know transformational social change comes from movements led by the people most impacted and marginalized. True diversity is core to what we do at all levels, and it starts with our Board of Directors…”
Latanya Mapp Frett, President and CEO of Global Fund for Women

“At FSD Africa, we are immensely proud that our Board of Directors is not only exceptionally well-qualified for the role but also majority female. Diversity is central to both our strategy and our values and we are already enacting various measures to ensure gender equity throughout our team as well as at Board level. We are fully supportive of the idea that there should be at least 30% female representation on our Board. We are committed to mainstreaming diversity through our organisation.”
Mark Napier - CEO, FSD Africa

This year, these organisations decided to commit towards mainstreaming gender diversity at the board level by joining the Board Diversity Charter. We call on others to follow suit. Progress towards gender diversity can only be achieved with intentionality and action on the part of all organisations.


Gender diversity starts with a commitment, sign up and join the charter today as a Signatory or Pledge Partner. Reach out to us to learn more and get involved.

2022 Highlight:

The European Union approves the “Women on Boards Law,” a groundbreaking mandate that requires listed companies in the EU to consist of at least 40% non-executive members from mid-2026.

The Roundup

The Institute of Director’s Centre for Corporate Governance has published a new report, ‘Governance and Innovation: Report of findings’, exploring the impact of the UK’s governance arrangements on innovation. The report identifies the most relevant factors that boards should consider so that the company’s detailed governance arrangements encourage rather than constrain innovation.
You’ve probably heard the analogy about the board chair being like an orchestra conductor. Matt Fullbrook of Fullbrook Board Effective thinks that’s incorrect. He has a different viewpoint - and it’s to do with dinner parties. On this episode of the Future Director’s podcast, Matt shares his analogy on how the role of a board chair is like being a great dinner host and explores what it takes to make board leadership better.
