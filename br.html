International Finance Corporation

Non-Executive Director


Industry: Healthcare

International Finance Corporation (IFC), a sister organisation of the World Bank and a member of the World Bank Group, is the largest global development institution focused on the private sector in developing countries. The Bank Group has set two goals for the world to achieve by 2030: end extreme poverty and promote shared prosperity in every country. IFC offers investment, advisory, and asset management services to encourage private-sector development in less developed countries.

IFC nominates directors to serve on the board of client companies. IFC is seeking to nominate 1 (one) Non-Executive Director to join the board of a healthcare (healthcare facilities) company with operations in Eastern Africa.

The application deadline for this role is 18 May at midnight 2023.

Lifestores Healthcare

Independent Non-Executive Director


Industry: Health Technology

Lifestores Healthcare is on a mission to democratise access to quality and affordable primary healthcare by creating the digital infrastructure needed to expand access. Their offerings include; a digital pharmaceutical marketplace, point-of-sale pharmacy software, and working capital financing to strengthen healthcare providers enabling them to provide better service to patients.

Their marketplace which enables pharmacies and hospitals to purchase high-quality medications at a 10-20% lower price than traditional wholesalers - has grown rapidly, registering over 10% of pharmacies in Nigeria. Within the next year, they aim to increase the number of registered pharmacies to 25% of those in Nigeria, impacting hundreds of thousands of patients per month. Lifestores is also exploring ways to utilise this network of pharmacies to support patients directly, similar to the model of PharmEasy in India.

Lifestores Healthcare is searching for 1 (one) Independent Non-Executive Director to join their board.

The application deadline for this role is 5 June at midnight 2023.

Canon Collins Trust

Executive Director


Industry: Education, Social Justice

Canon Collins Trust (CCT) is building a community of leaders, thinkers, activists, and social justice organisations. CCT’s work is underpinned by a spirit of solidarity rooted in their history in the anti-apartheid struggle. Its partnerships with the Legal Resources Centre, Equal Education and others help to empower some of South Africa’s most marginalised communities.

Through the Trust’s higher education and research funding, project grants and international events programme, the Trust’s unique approach cultivates a space where activism and research meet. Higher education is transformative for society, and Southern Africa’s development depends on strong leadership in key fields. Yet, while the benefits of higher education continue to rise, the costs of being left behind are also growing. The underfunding of postgraduate studies has become a looming crisis and a major impediment to transformation. This is why Canon Collins Trust supported over 4,000 individuals whose education addresses some of the most pressing issues in contemporary southern African society.

Canon Collins Trust is seeking 1 (one) Executive Director to provide vision, direction, leadership and management in formulating and delivering the Canon Collins Trust’s strategic objectives.

The application deadline for this role is 14 May at midnight 2023.
