GOVERNANCE PRINCIPLES




1. Board of Directors: The board is pivotal in governance, holding the responsibility for overseeing the management of the business and affairs of the company. Effective boards are composed of a balanced mix of skilled members who make decisions in the best interests of the corporation and its stakeholders.

2. Management Oversight: Corporate governance involves setting clear lines of accountability for management and monitoring their performance. This includes establishing metrics for management’s performance and aligning their incentives with the long-term goals of the company.

3. Shareholder Rights: Protecting the rights of shareholders and ensuring they receive key information about the company is essential. This includes the rights to vote on significant matters, such as the election of board members and approval of significant corporate actions.

4. Transparency and Disclosure: Companies must provide accurate and timely information regarding their financial status, performance, ownership, and governance. This transparency builds trust with stakeholders and ensures that the company is held accountable.

6. Risk Management: Identifying, assessing, and managing risks is a fundamental part of corporate governance. Effective risk management can prevent significant losses and ensure the company’s sustainability.

7. Stakeholder Interests: Good governance practices consider the interests of all stakeholders in decision-making processes. Engaging with stakeholders can help align business practices with societal expectations and contribute to the long-term success of the company.
